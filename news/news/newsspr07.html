SIN ON A STICK!!!
Diana careened out of control in a gluttonous shame spiral of 17 sins when her thriftiness and over-indulgence collided.
Diana was hot and hungry. She had planned to be out all day and had packed herself a lunch but, amazingly, couldn't find it in her massive-purse-of-no-return. Always thrifty, Diana decided to save some cash by getting something to eat at Food-4-Less. She walked up and down the aisles looking for something healthy to eat. She spotted a can of tuna and envisioned herself using her rusty old 72 hour kit can opener, but it all seemed like too much hard work. She was hungry NOW! She walked on. Eventually she found herself in the frozen desserts aisle and right smack in the middle of temptation. She cast a long, lusty look over the icy treats and then, suddenly, there they were; two of her very favorite things wrapped up together in one cellophane package � clearance fudgsicles. She was hot and hungry and they were cheap. I mean, really, frighteningly cheap. Just $1.50 for a pack of 18! Upon closer inspection she discovered why � they had been thawed out and then refrozen again. They were misshapen and molded to their flaccid wrappers but she was hungry and they were cheap, so she grabbed a pack. Could she eat 18 fudgsicles in one go? Was it a good idea at her age? "Oh well, I'm old I'm going to die soon anyway," she thought as she lined up to purchase the pack of withered "treats". In the car she bit into the first fudgsicle and immediately tasted the reason behind the reduction; it was freezer-burned and had a bitter tinge. It wasn't nice but she was hungry and they would start melting in a minute, so she finished the first and then opened another. Once she'd finished her fourth fudgsicle she hardly noticed the nasty refrozen texture and the bitter aftertaste. Somewhere around number six she actually began to enjoy the new flavor. By number eight she began to slow down a little. By 11 she had to try to convince herself that she was still hungry enough to polish them off. Number 12 was a bit hard to swallow but the remaining fudgsicles were beginning to melt and she had visions of them flooding the car � so she soldiered on! By number 14 she was suffering from severe brain freeze and began to worry about her health. What if she fell into some kind of sugar-induced diabetic coma and had to go to the hospital where they would cut her open and find a jumbo pack of fudgsicles inside?! She was going to die in shame! Still, she was almost done. By number 16 she was convinced that she had frostbite of the tongue. She stopped for a minute to consider the consequences. If she ended up with actual frostbite, would they have to amputate her tongue?! Number 17 and 18 were more "sludgsicle" than "fudgsicle" but she couldn't stop now, she was so close to the finish line! So she sucked down the 17th and then suddenly ground to a complete halt. She simply could not manage the last one � but she couldn't waste it either! In a frozen brainwave she decided to give the pitiful sack of sludge away to the first person to pass. She waved it out the window to several passers-by but there were no takers. Mothers hurried their children past the strange lady with brown, gooey stuff dripping from her frozen smile. Finally, she admitted defeat and reluctantly threw the last bag of toxic slush away. Then in a bloated, nauseous, stupor she drove off to the temple to repent. A few weeks later, and still feeling the effects, she was rummaging in her massive-purse-of-no-return and unexpectedly found her long-lost lunch! And even in its disgusting, decaying state, it still held more appeal for her than a Food-4-Less fudgsicle.

Local Students Win in 'Reflections' Competition
BY HARRY FRANKLIN
Two Double Churches Elementary School students are among 12 students in Muscogee County schools who are winners in the state Reflections competition sponsored by the Georgia Parent Teacher Association. But they will have to wait until a 1 p.m. Sunday reception in Atlanta to learn which place they won. Third-grader Kiriana Beal, 8, daughter of Scott and Melanie Beal, won at the district level in visual arts for a photograph and also has won at state. "It's a picture of my mom and little brother Joseph, 3, sitting opposite each other and mother tickling my brother," she said, adding she took the photo last year. "They are both laughing." She can't wait to find out how she placed in state competition. This is the 37th year the PTA has sponsored the Reflections competition for students in the arts from grades pre-K-12, said Dawn Small, Reflections chair for the Georgia PTA. Categories are judged by professionals in the state competition in the following categories: visual arts, photography, literature, musical composition, dance choreography and film and video production. Certificates, ribbons and some cash awards are presented. The state PTA contest has first, second and third-place finishers per category, plus honorable mention if judges choose. Four winners per category will compete in the national Reflections competition. Some 30,000 students entered the Reflections contest at their local school level and the work of about 1,800 district winners went to state, Small said.
