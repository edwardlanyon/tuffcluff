CHILD RUN OVER!
Thirteen Year Old Boy Hit By His Own One-Eyed Mother!
 Dallas Cluff is recovering after a brush with death at the hands of his own mother, Diana! After suffering from an aneurysm three years ago Diana was left with impaired vision in her left eye but was subsequently given the 'all clear' to drive from the DMV. So she continued to drive half blind without incident, but then disaster struck. "As we drove up to the church," says Diana, "Dallas opened the door before I had stopped the car. I felt a bump and knew that I had run over something. I asked what I'd hit and Weston said, 'It's okay Mom, it was just Dallas!' Luckily the tire had only caught Dallas's foot and he was okay, in fact he acted like nothing had happened at all! I don't know if he will hurt later or if it just doesn't hurt to have your foot run over. Oh well, win some lose some!"

 Karen Cluff, mild-mannered super mom, was driving along with her daughter at the speed limit (30 mph), minding her own business, when suddenly a car turned in front of her! She slammed on her brakes but it was too late - they collided. Her car was totalled and her air bag was deployed but she and Kaylynn were able to walk away from the accident with only a few bruises to the arms and chest. The other driver's insurance should cover everything, given that it was their fault, but that fact doesn't satisfy Ammon who was forced to take the minivan for his driver's test as a result of the incident.
Near Fatal Mrs.
 "Yesterday I was almost struck by a car pulling into my lane when I was about to go onto the freeway," says a terrified Diana. "Our car would have been smashed if I hadn't run off the road in self defense. The man must not have seen me coming as I have a grey car and it was cloudy and foggy. FARM LIVIN'
Chicken Report
 If you are planning a visit to the Cluff house this year, the main thing to remember is do not (I repeat, DO NOT) eat anything containing eggs! This year it should be especially hard to avoid since their chicken coop is bursting at the seams with 22 laying hens! But the hens aren't the real problem, the real problem is the rooster! Having a rooster makes each egg a serious gamble. Then why have a rooster? Dennis explains, "I bought 24 baby chicks last year because every year we lose half a dozen or more to racoons and possums. Also, usually about 25% of the batch ends up being roosters, but last year we only ended up with two! We ate one and that left 22 hens and one rooster. With the newly repaired chicken coop we didn’t lose any hens to predators and almost all of them lay eggs. Now we have eggs all over the place that we are trying to get rid of." The problem is that, with a rooster, some of those eggs are ticking time bombs. Dennis stars in the latest drama in a long list of bad egg stories; "Dad called me from work," Diana reports, "and said 'throw out all the boiled eggs, I just bit into one and I almost threw up!' Then the tone of his voice changed; he coughed, gagged, threw down the phone and ran out of the room yelling, 'I'm going to throw up!' I couldn't stop laughing!"

LIFESTYLES
Tree Trouble
 Having a troublesome Christmas tree is a long-standing Cluff tradition. For instance, Diana, who loves bushy trees, was always dissatisfied with what the forest had to offer and so every year had to lash two trees together to get the look she wanted. The problem then was how to disguise the join. She solved it with heavy flocking – a practice now banned in 39 states for being a crime against tastefulness. Everyone has their own idea of the perfect tree. Tiana, in direct opposition to her mother, prefers slim-line trees. “We bought our tree from the boy scouts in our ward,” says a ticked-off Tiana, “and the dumb thing was a bush instead of a tree. You know the ones that when you put the ornaments on they kind of stick out instead of hang? That's what I had. It was really weird for me because I'm used to trees with spaces between the branches. It worked out alright, but it died long before Christmas so I had to take it down on the 26th, and there were more needles on the floor afterward than there were on the tree. Next year I'm getting the tree I want. No more of this pity for the boy scouts stuff, unless they decide to sell decent trees.”

Cluff Numerolgy
 Dennis and Diana have had so many children it's often difficult to keep track of them. The problem is only compounded when you add their unusual, and often difficult to pronounce, names.
