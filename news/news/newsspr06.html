What in Tarnation?!
Clean-Living Nerds Rise to Power Armed Only With Fiddlesticks and Dag-nab-its!
 "Being a clean nerd is the new popular thing these days," says relieved mother of ten, Diana. "The Twins have made up clean words to say at school and now the whole school is copying them! It's nice to have that nasty, filthy language finally replaced." But contrary to popular belief this 'new' curse replacement trend is as old as the twins themselves. "It's actually just our twin language," reveals Weston with a smug grin. "Dallas and I would be taking to each other while our friends were around and they'd hear us using our twin-talk and then they just started copying us. It was pretty funny at first but then it got really weird because one morning I was just getting to school and some random girl saw me and suddenly shouted, 'what the foo?!' which was one of OUR things! And I didn't even know her!" As a phrase, 'what the foo?' is as complicated as it is peculiar. In twin-talk 'foo' is a not-so-clever variant of 'fool' - but then the clever bit comes in when it's twisted to be unrecognizable in an unlikely phrase (ie: 'what the foo?!'). This catchy phrase became so popular that it wasn't long before even elementary school children were heard saying it! The boys have revealed their top cussing substitutes as: foo & fooster (variants of fool), feak (variant of freak), ‘ate yee (hate you) and kibbin'(just kidding). Dallas admits that it's Weston's thing more than his but is still a keen participant and is proud that their language has gone mainstream. "It's pretty sweet," he says with a satisfied look in his eye, "but I always knew we'd be famous one day.” Then suddenly, “Hold on! Wait a sec! Did you say just say that Mom thinks we're nerds?!" He looks crestfallen and gives a teary, doe-eyed look that would make a rock weep, and then, "Haa! Kibbin! Better a nerd than a feak!" If you can come up with anything better, we'd love to hear it! TRAVEL & LEISURE
Mel's Second Trip to England
 "The night before arriving in Cornwall," Melanie remembers, "whilst en route, Edward had a small automobile nudging, resulting in a scratched bumper on our side, a cracked bumper and broken blinker light on theirs and a very bruised ego on Edward’s side. The rental car, which only had 200 miles on it, had to be written off as un-rentable after our little accident - though the damage was very slight. So Edward essentially “totaled” a car! Here are my favorite highlights from the places we visited:"
CORNWALL: "Tintagel, King Arthur’s birthplace and where he was crowned. Also, the birthplace of JoJo’s water obsession: JoJo almost stepped off the jagged coastal edge as he tried to dive into a little creek that immediately plunged into the craggy bay below. Boscastle, Dakota’s favorite town in Cornwall. We had a brisk walk to the North view point where you could probably fly your dog like a kite on his leash in the wind! Porthcurno Valley Telegraph Station. An unimpressed ticketing clerk couldn’t care less that Edward’s Grandfather was intimately involved with the station’s history. Cream tea at the Lanyon Farmhouse and a hike to the Lanyon Quoit. Tour of the Lanyon Manor House surrounded by fields of blooming daffodils. St. Michaels Mount with the giants heart turned to stone and the secret garden. Hiking down to Kynance Cove, Edward’s Favorite beauty spot in Cornwall. My legs felt so sore from hiking with Joseph on my hip. Land’s End and the Lizard, the Minack Theatre (which I loved), and the Merry Maidens (Dakota’s traditional Cornish rest stop for relieving himself). I absolutely loved the quality of the light in Cornwall, especially by the seaside. I wished there were Cluffs of Cornwall. We tried to hike to a point that was labeled “Carn Clough” on a map we had, but the natives told us it was just the bluffs and not named for the family. We went to Cornwall for the views but will go back for the food; the rock candy, Cornish cream teas, the pasties and especially the Cornish ice cream!"
THE COTSWOLDS (with Mark and Elizabeth too): "Hidcote Manor Gardens, where I saw my first real Haha. We had scones and jam and cream in the tea shop. I wondered what my house would look like covered in roses and wisteria. St. Peter’s church in Gloucestershire. After the church tour we had lunch with a chewy mint to finish off the meal, but it did more than just finish off the meal-it finished off my tooth too as I lost my temporary crown in an overzealous bite! Filkins, the market cross. The idea was that all transactions would be fair and honest if done near the cross. Stow-on-the-Wold had village stocks we tried to put Dakota and Joseph into. While backing up to get a perfect picture of the cottages at Arlington Row I nearly stepped into the stream behind me. Daisha’s favorite. Snowshill: It actually snowed while we crossed the tops of the lavender fields in Snowshill. Mark and Elizabeth took me to Statford-upon-Avon, also John Harvard’s birthplace. JoJo had two massive tantrums during our tour of Mary Arden’s home. The Tudor style is so “English”. All of the Cotswold’s remind me of storybook pictures."
DAYTRIPS (with Daisha): "Windsor: I nearly got run over by a car in Windsor (bloody tourist!) Knole, Sevenoaks: We took the children’s tour (American style) so that we didn’t have to read big academic descriptions of each room! Edward was unimpressed with this clever trick of ours! Poor me. English “dud” shampoo caused flat hair all day long (or maybe low water pressure!). Emmets Garden: The views were lovely, but all Joseph was interested in was the dog water bowl. We did an Easter egg hunt and received Dairy milk chocolates at end (Dakota’s favorite part). I tried to recognize where scenes of favorite film “A Room With a View” was shot. We dined on fish and chips at Harry Ramsdens finishing off the meal with Eton Mess! Penshurst Place and Gardens: Alice in Wonderland-like with long mazey hedges. The Great Hall had an ancient roof where the smoke filtered out through the rafters. Chartwell: Home of Sir Winston Churchill. We took a whirlwind tour through the house and his artist studio. The kitchen garden was fascinating, as was his daughter’s playhouse with the lovely view. Joseph loved the fish pond. Romantic Scotney Castle: The clerk in the gift shop arched an eyebrow at our whirlwind tour of the gardens. They deserve a thorough examination in the future. Edwards fav.Ightham Mote: Tudor style tiny house surrounded by a mote. Reminds me of Snow White. One garden even has a wishing well. Bodiam Castle: we climbed to the top of a turret to enjoy the birds eye view. It's home to a huge tree with “spooky” branches that brushed Joseph's face. It has a mote full of ducks that terrorized Joseph. He was stampeded by them and to this day would rather throw rocks than bread to ducks. Sissinghurst: My favorite garden, just 30 minutes from Daisha’s house. It wasn’t blooming much but the bare branches let me see how things were pruned and staked and tied. I need to see it in June."

Danger Zone: FIRE ALERT!!
 "It was open-burn season," says fire enthusiast, Diana, "so we had a big fire in the field all day. Dusk came and when I opened the back door I saw a horrible, thick cloud of smoke everywhere. Dallas was standing in the smoke and when I asked if the burn fire was out of control, he smiled calmly and said it was nothing. Not believing him, I dashed down the stairs and saw to my horror that a fire was burning in the needles under the big fir tree that towers over our house. I screamed, 'FIRE! Get a hose!' Dallas kept saying it was nothing while Weston threw a pile of needles on the fire to smother it. I kept screaming for them to get the hose but they just laughed at me and said that they had built the fire to get warm and it could be put out easily. Suddenly, the bed of needles began to flare up only a couple of feet from the house! Weston, who had a shovel, attempted to put it out but just ended up shoveling hot coals all over the needles. I ran for the hose. I couldn't believe that two boys who were almost Eagle Scouts and who had handled fire since they were toddlers were about to burn the house down. They just kept laughing, not believing me. I soaked the fire for a half a hour and made the boys shovel all the coals out to bare ground. When everything settled down I went up to the field to check on the other fire. I heard a lot of cracking and popping and turned back toward the house where I saw what looked like a huge, beautiful moonrise, all golden and fantastic, then realized it wasn't the moon but a house fire! I just knew the twins had burned down the house but as I ran back I saw that it wasn't our home but our neighbor's house across the creek. I had Dennis call the fire department. We heard the fire engines driving in circles and getting lost as they tried to find the burning house. The twins sat on top of our roof to watch the fire. After it was all over Dallas came to me and said that this was the best lesson on the best day he could have had for burning down a house. He saw that it only took about ten minutes for the house to be completely engulfed in flames. As it turned out, the fire was a new barn that had been built to house marijuana plants. The hot lights to help them grow had caught fire and the owners had fled to avoid being caught by the police."

Celebration Cake Tip
 Have a fantastic birthday, wedding or special occasion with a picture cake. Here's how to do it:
1. Scan a photo. You could use pictures of ancestors or pictures from the family album.
2. Print the photo on your printer with EDIBLE paper and ink (buy it at computer supply shops).
3. Place the finished print on top of a flat iced cake and decorate around the edges with a tube of frosting.
4. Add iced or real flowers, toys, candy or decorations for the theme. For example; numbers, sport balls, ballet dancers, tiny babies for a shower or things for a hobby the person has.
*Also, the bakery at your supermarket will often do the whole thing for you if you take a photo to them.
