Cruising Maine
Ten Wild Kids, One Fantasic Voyage
"Listen up swabbies," the invitation began. "Take the oath now and sign it in blood! If ye can't, then stay behind with the other yeller bellied landlubbers drudging out their days respiring flatulent from behind a mule dragging a dusty, broken plow in endless circles in the quest of meager seasonal crops. Then let your mind wander to the brave lassies and laddies breath'n the clean salt air of your ancestral home; a fresh breeze on their cheeks, sails cracking, bound for exotic harbors, new worlds and lobster for $4 a pound!" Nine lucky TuffCluff kids (Dallas, Weston, Nial, Ammon, Kaylynn, Braden, Jasmine, Cage and Dakota) responded to the invitation of a lifetime and got to go sailing off the coast of Maine with their very brave Uncle Randy and his daughter, Emma. Their journey consisted of learning to sail, exploring, eating, shopping, stargazing, eating, participating in various exciting nautical diversions (knot tying, tacking, swabbing etc) and eating. High points included stirring up bioluminescence, exploring historical sites in Boston and dolphin spotting while low points included a few mild cases of seasickness and a nasty clogged-toilet incident. The kids had too much fun to feel any pangs of homesickness, much to the chagrin of their parents, and felt the trip was over all too soon. All in all, it was a fantastic experience for every lad and lass.

Bible Shocker
 Three Teenage Boys Discover Raunchy Bible Stories
In their short lives, Nial, Dallas and Weston have spent more time in scripture study than a monastery full of elderly Monks with sunshine allergies. However, it's only recently that they've discovered the seedier side of the scriptures because, up until now, they've miraculously managed to avoid the Old Testament. "We've been reading the Bible as a family," says religious mother of ten, Diana, "and our teenaged boys have been shocked by some of the things in its text. They were most appalled by the rated R stories of Sodom and Gomorrah and Lot's daughters. They kept saying 'sick!' and Nial kept wanting to stop reading [though, to be fair, this is nothing out of the ordinary]. Later, I heard him asking his friends, 'Have you read the Bible lately?!'." The Cluffs are now considering imposing a curfew on the Old Testament in an effort to curb the boy's newfound fondness for sneaking the book into their rooms after midnight for a little late night reading!!
Twins Attacked By Wild Animal
 One hot day, Dallas, Weston and Nial decided to go camping in the wilderness together. They set out for the Klamath River but, being true Cluffs, left late. Their problems were compounded after stopping to get Taco Bell on the way, which not only made them later but also gave them gas. They arrived well after dark, hot and stinky. They were about to pitch their tent when they saw an old shack with some wild people nearby and reluctantly decided it might be better to stay in their tiny car despite the smell. Weston drew the short straw and had to sleep in the back seat while Nial and Dallas fully reclined into his three-inches of space. Needless to say it was not most comfortable of nights. The next morning the twins headed straight for the Klamath river to swim. Nial decided to stay dry and fish from the riverbank. "Nial was fishing," recalls the boy's nervous mother, Diana, "when suddenly the twins began screaming like they were being eaten by a crock! They had come face to face with a big swimming snake in the river. They thought it was a rattle snake. The twins separated. Weston headed for shore and jumped out right into stinging nettle. Dallas took off in a wild upstream swim with the snake just behind him." Nial said the twins swam like they were on the swim team and screamed like they were six year old girls - he enjoyed the view. But the twins didn't enjoy the experience and have crossed the Klamath River off their list of favorite places to camp.

DIANA'S TIPS
Here, wedged between 'Lifestyles' and 'Bizarre-o World', Diana imparts some of her experience and wisdom to help with everyday problems.
Marriage Tip
 "Marriage comes with all sorts of differences that we have to accept even though we feel like putting a rotten tomato in their lunch box because they aren't like us! Everyone should be glad that we are all so different, but if you aren't, here's a tip on getting your husband to change. Serve a nice meal, then ask if it would be okay to study lessons on life together for 5 minutes each day after dinner. Start with the biggest problems you have. Use books from the library or magazines so it won't be lessons from "you". Kiss after the lesson and say thanks for the study time together. Serve dessert after the lesson, as it keeps your partner at the table for the lesson. If they have a bad attitude, next time add ex-lax or kidney dye to their dinner."
BIZARRE WORLD
"Surprise" Birthday
 This August Dennis turned 66 and had a "surprise" birthday party - not surprise in the traditional sense, just bizarre and unexpected. Weeks earlier Diana had asked what he wanted for his birthday and he had very sensibly thought it would be nice to replace their filthy, old, splintering, cider-barrel hot tub. Unfortunately, Diana disapproved on the grounds that nothing was bigger or better than the one they already owned. (In actual fact, even a cow trough filled with sulfur water is nicer!) So he suggested a laser pen for pointing out the stars to the family, but Diana had heard that people flash the pens in pilot’s faces and get arrested, so she shot down that idea too. Finally he settled on something that couldn't possibly cause controversy, a picture frame with a photo of his family for his office. When Dennis's birthday finally rolled around and he opened his presents he got a photo album (not a frame) without photos, a shirt that was two sizes too small and a collection of Halloween sundries; a Dracula mask, a warty troll mask and a scull goblet. There may be someone out there who would prefer a Halloween mask to a hot tub, but we’ve never met them. At least it was an unforgettable birthday!
