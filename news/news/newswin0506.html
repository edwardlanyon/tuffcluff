    • Danger Zone: FLOOD ALERT!!
     "We had masses of rain," reports Diana the disaster magnet, "and the creek flooded worse than ever before. I rose from my sick-bed to get the three heavy benches by the fire-pit up to the lawn and rope the garden furniture to the trees. Of course Dad and the boys were not at home, I had to do all the work by my sick self. The water was so high that it went over the trampoline. When it looked like the creek was about to flood the house, several young men showed up to help us move our valuables to higher ground. They also saved our poor drowning chickens. I wanted to sandbag the house but was told to forget it as we couldn't do it fast enough to save our home. Finally people started yelling at me to leave as the water was all the way up our second porch step. We all left together (people helped drive our cars, vans and motorhomes away) but I suddenly remembered that I hadn't turned off the shop power and went back to do it. Meanwhile, Dad had come from work and was standing there looking at the state of things. I asked if he could divert the flood from our home with his priesthood power. He didn't hesitate. Within 15 minutes the rain stopped and the water receded a foot. Then, when the rain came again a bit later, the county discovered that logs were plugging up the bridge on Dead Indian Road and pulled them loose which made the water go down even more. The Lord works in natural ways, like inspiring men to check the bridges. The creek has settled down now but still looks like a fast flowing river. It's wider now which means that our hot dog area isn't as wide anymore. Oh well, win some, lose some."
    Shark Boy
     Nemo may have lured the name 'Shark Bait', but when it comes to the real gill, Braden may have it hooked! Braden got permission to skip school one Tuesday and went deep sea fishing with his friend Darron and his father. They got all their tack together and set out for their favorite spot in Bandon. Only an hour later they were in the water, lines set and hoping for the best. They waited with no luck for most of the morning and were getting a bit crabby when suddenly Braden got a bite! A wave of excitement curled through him as he quickly grabbed the pole and pulled and reeled but to no avail. Thinking he must have hooked a log and wanting to get free of the debris, he tugged and tugged and tugged! But when the log tugged back, he knew he had a live fish who wanted to be left right where he was. Braden's no guppy, but it was all just too much, so he handed his pole to his friend but even he was floundering, so they passed the pole to his dad who reeled in the beast. There on the end of the line was Braden's first ocean catch; a four foot baby shark! When we asked him why he did it Braden's cool remark was, "Oh, just for the halibut!"
    Twins Earn 'Brush With Death' Merit Badge
     "The twins were all hot to go with on a snow campout with the scouts," says Diana. "They spent $200 on snow pants, a sled, hand warmers, top ramen and candy bars. They were going on a six mile hike to a cabin near Crater Lake. It was snowing heavily and the light was about gone when they set out. The older boys immediately took off, leaving the younger boys (including the twins) and scout leaders behind. They tried to follow the older boys' footprints but the trail split, so three groups of scouts were wandering around in a dark snowstorm. The twin's group eventually lost the trail and began to hike in circles. Hours passed and they were still hiking. They couldn't find the cabin and the snow was now up to their waists. One leader went ahead to try and find the older boys but couldn't see through the blinding snow and got lost. The younger boys found him crawling in the snow and in bad shape. Around midnight, after hours of hiking in bad conditions, they began to feel that they weren't going to make it. Dallas said he about passed out three times. Weston said he didn't know if he was up or down because things were so bad that they couldn't think straight. They had frozen hands, faces and hair, but their new clothes kept their bodies warm. A couple of the boys on the hike only wore sweatshirts! At about 2 am they finally got back to their cars (which were buried under the snow) and were able to pack the half-dead boys inside and take them home to thaw out. They got home around 3 am. When it was light, the scout leaders called Search & Rescue to find the older boys. The rescue team set out on their snowmobiles but had a hard time in the ten and twelve foot mounds of snow. Eventually they found the cabin and dug a trench down to the door. When they opened the door to make the rescue the boys inside were baffled. They hadn't even realized that there was trouble. They admitted that finding the cabin was difficult and that two of them got lost, but never realized how bad it was for the others. It turned out that the twin's group got within a few yards of the cabin but couldn't see it, as it was under a huge snowdrift. Our boys hate snow camping now and never want to experience it again. They were exhausted and dehydrated and would have starved if it weren't for all the candy bars they bought. I wondered why the twins didn't build a snow-cave instead of going another six miles back to the car. The boys said the leaders didn't want to stop, they just kept thinking they could find the cabin or the cars. They had a near-death experience, but were spared. Oh well, win some, lose some."

    THIS WEEK: Special Food Storage Issue!
    Food Storage Tips
     Ten years ago I wrote a survival manual called "Keeping Up With The Times". The following are some top tips from the manual:
  • Car Food Storage Keep a backpack in the trunk of your car filled with food (crackers, dried fruit, a few cans of tuna with a can opener, etc), water, a sleeping bag for warmth, a good book for long waits and containers and tissue for human waste. I learned this when living in LA with long waits on the freeways and in our mountain freeways in Oregon when stormy weather causes disruption now and then. If you are prepared you won't have to seek food, warmth and a bathroom and can stay in your car. ALWAYS stay in your car in a bad snow storm because it is the only way to be found. Most travelers leave their cars and then run out of food and warmth.
  • Winter Food Storage I always buy about four 10 lb bags of potatoes and have a big 25 lb sack of beans on hand for the winter. I buy big bags of flour for lots of bead and scones, or keep a wheat grinder to grind by hand. I store 50 lbs of oatmeal in the chest freezer or dry pack it. I keep a tomato plant growing inside by a south facing window and pollinate it with a tiny paint brush (see my new, fast tomato salad recipe). I keep a quart jar with a screen on top in the kitchen window with wheat sprouts for salads. Try deer this time of year. Make lots of jerky over a campfire. I know how to smoke it, if anyone wants to know. Or buy meat on sale and deep freeze it. I buy 4 or 5 extra turkeys at Thanksgiving and stick them in freezer for later. It is good to keep some extra food at home with grocery stores being the main source of food and natural disasters increasing everywhere these days. Don't be caught without food or water when the stores are empty. Fill all empty jars and jugs with water and add a capful of bleach to keep the water safe for later use.
  • Fresh Summer Food I plant pots outside the house for broccoli, chard, tomatoes, brussel sprouts, strawberries, rhubarb, green onions, yellow onions and radishes. I keep a gallon of homemade yogurt going every week to make fresh blackberry, banana and rhubarb yogurt smoothies. Cattails are edible if you have to resort to wild food. The roots are good, like potatoes, and the young tails are like corn. You can find a lot of salad material in your own backyard. Get to know wild foods. I still want a goat for meat, cheese, butter, cottage cheese and milk, but don't have one at this point in time.
