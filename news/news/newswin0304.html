BABY BONANZA!
Two births down, but still two more to go! The Cluff girls are going to town with pregnancy in 2004!
Melanie & Baby Joseph: Baby Joseph Allen Beal was born on Monday, February 23 2004 at 3:06 p.m. to a delight family. He weighed 7 pounds 15.6 ounces and was 20 inches long. Joseph was taken two weeks early due to slight complications with the pregnancy but all went well with the eight-hour birth (a significant improvement on the 36 hours she was in labor with Soren). Joseph is Scott and Melanie's third child and Dennis and Diana's tenth grandchild. The proud parents couldn't be happier; "He is so perfect," says Joseph's elated mother, Melanie. "We are all so enchanted with him. He arrived Monday afternoon, and none too soon either! It was only eight hours of labor, but when that kind of pain is involved, time is of the essence. We named him Joseph Allen, the Allen part after Scott. The kids are so preciously gentle with him; it is one of my favorite things to watch. We went in on Monday the 23rd for an induction, I asked for and got an epidural, which lasted just till I hit transition, then it "floated". By the time the second epidural was in, 45 painful minutes later, I was at a 10 and ready to go. But his bulbous head didn't want to come out so after the vacuum, forceps and the vacuum again, we finally managed to coax him out. All was forgiven as I held him for the first time. Scott cut the cord. He was brilliant through it all and I can't stop thanking him for being so wonderful." Melanie's youngest brothers, 12-year-old twins Dallas and Weston, took pictures of Joseph to school to show everyone--announcing that they had become uncles for the 10th time! Neither the kids nor the teachers believed them.
Indie & Baby Quints: After lumbering around for weeks in a painful and swollen state, Indian, Tiana and Hyrum's beautiful chocolate Min Pin finally gave birth to five puppies. She had four red and one black babies--all female except one. Sadly one died just a couple of days after the birth. All are walking around now with their eyes open and are getting into everything. Tiana and Hyrum couldn't be happier.
Tiana & Baby Girl: Tiana is just a couple of months away from having her first child, a baby girl she is hoping to name Cayanna. She and Hyrum are very excited and have been working hard to get their house and property ready for the new addition. Heavily pregnant Tiana has even learned to operate some of the heavy digging and loading equipment used to level their hill in preparation for their new house. Diana, Tiana's mother, is stunned by her strength, "Tiana drives the loader and big equipment in rain and snow and she'd probably work a 10 hour day with Hyrum if she wasn't pregnant. She is as strong as a man and really upbeat." Tiana is due mid May.
Natasha & Baby ???: Natasha and her partner, Steve, are expecting a baby in July. "Steve and I wanted to have a kid together," says Tasha, "but we didn't expect to be so lucky so soon. I am exited, but it's not like the first time. I'm not really that worried about anything and it's much more annoying to hear advice from people this time than last time. I know I shouldn't let it get to me but I did a pretty good job with Cage by myself...how much harder can it be with someone else actually helping me?" This will be Tasha's second child. Cage, her first child, will be ten when the baby is born.

SPECIAL INVESTIGATION: Theft, Gluttony, Rodents & Addiction
 For years Diana Cluff, seemingly self-sacrificial mother of ten, has been taking a hefty 85% "tithe" from the boys' Halloween candy and squirreling it away in the unnavigable depths of her closet. She claims that the confiscated candy will be used to stuff stockings at Christmas, but the sad reality is that the bulk of this shameful hoard is eaten up by Diana herself weeks before the holidays even arrive. By Christmastime, what little candy is left is usually too dangerous to eat. Not because it's old and out-of-date but because Diana's closet has recently been found to contain an usually large amount of rodents. The high-energy carbohydrate treats sustain not just the rat-infested closet but also the entire population of rodents for miles around. Diana refuses to be blamed for Eastern Ashland's rodent problems and resents the implication that she is greedy and crafty. In her own defence she has revealed that Shira (a convicted sugarholic) sneaks into the hoard at regular intervals as well. Far from greedy, Diana even believes her efforts are altruistic and that by taking a tithe she is saving the boys from cavities and her husband from dental bills. But the boys are unmoved by Diana's defense and just want their candy back. And so what began as an allegedly innocent, even angelic, act has now turned into a damaging and despicable lose-lose scheme creating not only familial resentment and suspicion but also hyperactive, diabetic rats. YOUR VIEWS
A Thin Line Between Love & Hate by Shira Cluff
When I live at home I wake up each morning only to look forward to eating a small bowl of stale oatmeal and skim milk, which hardly sustains my hunger until lunchtime. At lunch I go eat an apple (which I have to get by literally climbing one of the apple trees in our yard--then ones you can easily pluck off the ground are definitely inedible--but the risk is never worth it because I end up biting into the nastiest, brown, putrid rot of a worm's hole because not one of the apples in our yard is worm-hole-free) or maybe have a little bean and onion soup, only enough to fill my shrivelled, gaseous stomach until dinner when, if I am lucky, I fix myself an egg. That is ALL that there is to offer at my house that fits in with my diet, if you don't count what I can salvage out of the mulch bucket. When I am really lucky Daisha and I will go to Wal-Mart where I will stock up on sugar free candies and chocolates only to be wishing I hadn't, hours later, when my aching stomach, making all sorts of strange and unearthly noises, bubbles and I sit on the pot with the worst diarrhea anyone anywhere is truly able to experience. That's only if I get really lucky, as I've already said. Don't get me wrong, actually I've never eaten so well and been so skinny at the same time. Plus the yard work really subtracts in places where the lack of food and nutrition misses. However, the funny thing is, as I sit here in Portland, after having afforded a day's digestional workout of omelettes, buttered toast, hash browns, fish 'n' chips, bangers 'n' mash, sodas, muffins, chocolate cream pie and candy, I really do realize how much better I feel when the day is done, all the nails have been pried from an endless stack of ply wood, hauled to the fire-pit, and are ready for a hot dog roast, the last of the rotted apples have been collected for applesauce, the watering is finished and Rich Wilford took no part in it, the condiments have been removed, then replaced (something they call cleaning the fridge) though I can't tell the difference, and I lie in bed having eaten just enough to keep me alive, food that just barely tastes good enough that I don't start searching for room and board elsewhere, yet at the same time gives me the satisfaction of feeling tired at 8pm, having the energy to wake up before noon, of being able to sleep through Dallas's snoring, of actually witnessing my body, first hand, self-depreciate - my Cluff thighs..., disintegrate, and that, is the best feeling of all. At this instant I am so sick to my stomach from eating so much junk food; I truly wish I was at home eating that delicious bean and onion soup and happily snug in my 0-degree sleeping bag. I am being completely sincere.
