"COMETS: THE FIERY FLYING FURNACES OF SPACE"
by Soren Beal
Well, actually, comets are cold, really cold. Comets are objects that orbit the sun. When they are orbiting the sun, and when they get close enough to it, the sun's heat melts the comet so that a tail is formed. It is like a huge snowball. Solar energy heats the comet, which creates gas and dust that forms the comet's tail. Some comets have two tails: a dust tail, and a gas tail. The dust tail is more yellow than the gas tail. The gas tail is bluish in color. Some comets can be seen with the naked eye when they get close enough to the earth. However, most comets can only be seen with large telescopes. We cannot always predict when a comet will appear, but Halley's comet is an exception. This comet appears every 74 to 79 years, and will be seen again in the year 2061.
When I was four years old, my Mom and Dad watched the comet named Hale-Bop as we drove through the mountains of Washington and Oregon. It was during winter, and the skies were crystal clear. I, personally, do not remember seeing the comet. I was asleep in the back seat of the car.
